At first glance, AI safety and environmental sustainability might seem like separate concerns. One deals with algorithms and alignment; the other with ecosystems and emissions. But I've come to see them as deeply connected.

## Shared Patterns

Both fields grapple with similar challenges:

**Long-term thinking**: Both require us to consider consequences that extend far beyond immediate returns. An unsafe AI might cause problems years from now. Carbon emitted today affects climate for decades.

**Collective action problems**: Neither can be solved by individuals alone. We need coordination, governance, and shared standards.

**Exponential dynamics**: Both involve systems that can change rapidly and nonlinearly. A feedback loop in climate or in AI capability can accelerate beyond our ability to control.

**Uncertainty and risk**: We're making decisions under deep uncertainty in both domains, which requires humility and precaution.

## Tensions

There are also tensions between them. Training large AI models requires significant computational resources. The race to develop more capable AI can override concerns about energy efficiency.

Yet AI also offers tools for sustainability: optimizing energy grids, modeling climate systems, accelerating materials research for clean energy.

## A Unified Approach

I believe we need frameworks that consider both concerns together:

- How do we develop AI that's both aligned with human values *and* environmentally responsible?
- How do we govern powerful technologies in ways that account for multiple types of long-term risk?
- How do we foster research cultures that value caution alongside innovation?

These questions don't have easy answers, but asking them together might lead to better solutions than treating them separately.
