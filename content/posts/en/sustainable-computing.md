Every time we load a webpage, send an email, or train a machine learning model, we consume energy. Data centers already account for about 1-2% of global electricity use, and that number is growing.

## The Hidden Costs

It's easy to think of digital services as "clean" because we don't see the infrastructure behind them. But consider:

- A single large language model training run can emit as much carbon as five cars over their lifetimes
- Streaming video accounts for a significant portion of internet traffic
- The production of devices requires rare earth minerals and generates e-waste

## Principles for Sustainable Computing

Here are some principles I try to follow:

### 1. Efficiency by Design

Write efficient code. Choose lightweight solutions when they suffice. A static website uses far fewer resources than a dynamic one with the same content.

### 2. Longevity

Use devices longer. Resist the upgrade cycle when your current tools work well. The most sustainable device is the one you already have.

### 3. Mindful Consumption

Be intentional about digital consumption. Do you need to stream in 4K? Does that meeting require video?

### 4. Advocate for Change

Support policies and companies that prioritize sustainability. Push for renewable energy in data centers.

## A Path Forward

Technology isn't inherently unsustainable—but our current practices often are. By being thoughtful about our choices and advocating for systemic change, we can help build a digital future that respects planetary boundaries.
